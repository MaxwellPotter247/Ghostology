Ghosts of The Former
Idaho Territory

March 3, 1863 President Abraham Lincoln signed an act of congress creating the Idaho Territory which encompassed the modern states of Idaho, Montana, and Wyoming. Life was not easy though in the newly formed territory. Lawlessness, crime, and hostilities with the area’s Native Americans were common, but this did not stop thousands of people from many places, cultures, and ethnicities migrating to the Idaho Territory. Most were looking for riches in the mining industries of the time, others followed the expansion of the railroads, and some were searching for place to call home. What every migrant discovered were the rugged landscape of the desert west and the unforgiving heights of the Rocky Mountains. Violence, disease, and death were frequently found in the territory. The dangers of the 1800s and early 1900s were harsh in the Idaho Territory. Stories of accidents, illnesses, and murders seem to fill its early cemeteries. From mining disasters, smallpox epidemics, homicidal bandits, and vigilante mobs there were many ways territorial residents could die any given day. It should be no surprise that the ghost towns, railways, and scenic landscapes of the former Idaho Territory are rumored to be haunted. The spirits of the former residents don’t seem to want to forgotten. Scott V. Brian, paranormal investigator and trained archaeologist has spent over a decade researching and investigating some of the most haunted places in the former Idaho Territory. He is excited to continue telling his most terrifying findings in Ghostology: Tales from a Decade of Paranormal Investigation, Volume Two. This next volume of the Ghostology series is dedicated to the haunting tales from the states of the former Idaho Territory. Haunted Youth

Haunting tales of the paranormal inspired by real ghostly encounters! Is the Llorona real? Are Ouija boards dangerous? Can ghosts follow you home? If you crave ghost stories based on real hauntings, then Ghostology: Based on Actual Events will take you on thrilling adventures full of ghosts, hauntings, and other supernatural occurrences. Learn about a real Llorona haunting from Mexico, a haunted dam, the dangers of playing with a Ouija board, a living ghost, and a ghostly neighborhood friend. All of the paranormal activities in this collection of stories are based on real people and their experiences written in story form for maximum enjoyment. In 2007, author and paranormal investigator, Scott Brian, founded the Southeast Idaho Paranormal Organization. For over a decade, he has investigated reported hauntings all over Idaho and surrounding states. Beyond his own research, Scott has also collected numerous first-hand accounts of ghostly encounters and paranormal activity. Now in this series, he will share some of the most electrifying and terrifying tales told to him by the actual witnesses to the paranormal. Ghostology: Based on Actual Events is a collection of real paranormal experiences, fictionalized and retold in story form. Though the names and some details have been changed, the ghostly events described in each story have been written to reflect true encounters with the unknown. The first book in this series, Haunted Youth, focuses on stories that center around children who have experienced ghostly visitations or even demonic disturbances. The children and their interactions with the supernatural range from imaginary friends, to ghostly disturbances, and even the occult. The stories in this book are for everyone of all ages who are looking for the ghost stories of children. But beware, because even though characters are fiction, the ghosts are real!
